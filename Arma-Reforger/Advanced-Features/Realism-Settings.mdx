# Configuring Realism Settings in Arma Reforger  

Realism settings enhance immersion by simulating real-world mechanics. This guide will show you how to adjust settings like ballistics, damage models, and AI behavior to create a more authentic experience.

## Overview  

Key realism settings include:  
- Ballistics: Bullet drop, wind effects, and ricochets.  
- Damage Models: Realistic injury and vehicle damage.  
- AI Behavior: Intelligent enemy tactics and reactions.  

---

## Steps to Configure Realism Settings  

### Step 1: Adjust Ballistics  
Enable realistic ballistics in the `server.cfg` file:  
```properties
# Enable realistic ballistics
realistic-ballistics=true
```

---

### Step 2: Customize Damage Models  
Adjust damage settings for players and vehicles:  
```properties
# Example: Increase vehicle damage realism
vehicle-damage=realistic
player-damage=realistic
```

---

### Step 3: Enhance AI Behavior  
Use Enforce Script to customize AI tactics:  
```javascript
// Example: Add flanking behavior to AI
setAICombatBehavior(enemyGroup, "flank");
```

## Troubleshooting Common Issues  

### Issue: Unrealistic Behavior  
- **Cause**: Incorrect realism settings or outdated mods.  
- **Solution**:  
  1. Double-check realism settings in `server.cfg`.  
  2. Update mods to ensure compatibility with realism features.  

### Issue: Performance Impact  
- **Cause**: High realism settings increase resource usage.  
- **Solution**:  
  1. Optimize settings for performance (e.g., reduce AI complexity).  
  2. Upgrade hosting resources if necessary.  

---

## Best Practices  

- Gradually introduce realism settings to avoid overwhelming players.  
- Test realism settings thoroughly before deploying them to production.  
- Gather player feedback to refine realism features.
